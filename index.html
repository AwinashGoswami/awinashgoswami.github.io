    "John is 50 years old, can his son be 65?" Evaluating NLP Models' Understanding of Feasibility

    Demonstrated the limitations of large language models (LLM) like GPT3 and T5 by creating numerical feasibility data set. Created data set and experimentation to show that the GPT3 has accuracy of just 19%. Also, demonstrated the knowledge ingestion inability of such models. Paper is published at EACL 2023.

  • Instruction Learning

    May 17, 2023
    Instruction Tuned Models are Quick Learners

    This research highlights instruction tuning in language models, showcasing their impressive sample efficiency. In single task learning, they excel with only 25% of the data, while in multi-task learning, they achieve state-of-the-art results with just 6% of the data. This approach improves sample efficiency, and pre-finetuning with instructions consistently enhances performance by around 4%. However, challenges arise in tasks like question rewriting and title generation with instruction tuning.

    Created a Masked Language Model (MLM, BERT) for recovery of variable from decompiled source code. Comparing pre-training performance of original code and decompiled code and effect of selective variable masking using a Constrained Masked Language Model (CMLM) for performance improvements.

    InstructABSA: Instruction Learning for Aspect Based Sentiment Analysis

    InstructABSA improves Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA) by including diverse examples during training, surpassing previous state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods in Term Extraction, Sentiment Classification, and Sentiment Pair Extraction on Sem Eval datasets, maintaining a strong generalization with just 50% of the training data.

  • Under review (NAACL)

    Oct 30, 2023
    TarGEN: Targeted Data Generation with Large Language Models

    TarGEN, a seedless strategy, generates high-quality synthetic datasets using LLMs, outperforming originals by 1-2% in SuperGLUE tasks. With instruction tuning, it achieves 84.54% on synthetic data versus 81.49% on originals, maintaining diversity. T5-3B pre-finetuned on TarGEN tops the OpenLLM leaderboard by 4.14%, providing an efficient benchmark solution.
